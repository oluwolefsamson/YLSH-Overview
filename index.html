The Problem

Youth programs are under-served by technology

Across Africa, thousands of government ministries, foundations, NGOs, and academic institutions run youth programs that reach millions of young people each year. Yet nearly all of them manage registrations over email, track attendance in Excel, issue paper certificates that are trivially forged, and have no way to measure their programme's impact beyond headcounts.

The result is wasted funding, opaque accountability, and a broken experience for participants who receive no lasting benefit from their engagement.

"We spent three weeks after every summit manually emailing PDF certificates — half of which bounced. We had no idea who actually attended until we checked the photos."

— Programme Coordinator, Federal Ministry of Youth Development
  • No verified identity
    Duplicate registrations, ghost attendees, and certificate fraud are rampant.
  • No continuity
    Participants attend one event and disappear — there is no ongoing engagement channel.
  • No data for funders
    Organisations cannot demonstrate impact with auditable evidence, limiting repeat funding.

The Solution

One platform. Every touchpoint.

YLSH consolidates every stage of a youth programme — from first registration to post-event opportunity access — into a single, auditable platform. Organisations deploy it in hours; participants use it from any device; administrators get real-time analytics without writing a single report.

Identity & Access Control

Participants register once with NIN-based identity verification. JWT authentication, role-based access control (RBAC), password reset flows, and full audit logs ensure that only verified, authorised individuals access programme resources.

Event Management & Attendance

End-to-end event lifecycle: create, publish, manage registrations, waitlists, and cancellations. On the day, QR-code scanning confirms attendance in real time and flags duplicate check-ins, eliminating proxy attendance fraud.

Certificates & QR Verification

PDF certificates are generated asynchronously and delivered to participants automatically. Each certificate contains a unique QR code that links to a tamper-proof verification page — enabling employers and institutions to confirm authenticity instantly.

Mentorship Network

Participants browse a curated directory of mentors spanning Tech & Engineering, Entrepreneurship, Policy & Governance, and more. They book one-on-one video sessions, receive reminders, and build an ongoing record of their mentorship journey — visible to both parties.

Learning Resources

A structured library of videos, PDFs, and articles covering leadership, digital skills, entrepreneurship, policy, and grant writing. Participants track progress per resource; organisers see aggregate completion rates to measure programme effectiveness.

Opportunities Board

Jobs, internships, grants, and scholarships are curated and surfaced directly in each participant's dashboard. Applications are submitted in-platform; participants track status (under review, shortlisted, offered) without leaving the ecosystem.

Admin & Analytics Dashboard

Programme administrators get a real-time control panel: user management, event oversight, verification queues, and engagement KPIs. Super-admins can govern multiple organisations, manage role assignments, and export audit-ready reports for funders.

Participant Profile & Verification

Every participant builds a persistent, verified profile — profile photo, bio, NIN verification, and a portable record of events attended, certificates earned, sessions completed, and opportunities applied for. Completion scores drive discoverability with mentors and event organisers.

Technology

Enterprise-grade infrastructure, purpose-built for scale

YLSH is architected for reliability, security, and rapid iteration. The decoupled frontend–backend design allows each layer to scale independently as programme volume grows.

  • Next.js + TypeScript Frontend
    Server-side rendering for performance and SEO. MUI component library for a consistent, accessible UI across all devices.
  • Node.js / Express REST API
    Stateless, JWT-secured API with input validation, rate limiting, Helmet security headers, and structured logging.
  • Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)
    Four distinct roles — Participant, Mentor, Admin, Super-Admin — each with scoped permissions and dedicated dashboards.
  • NIN Identity Verification
    Nigeria National Identity Number verification ensures each registered profile maps to a unique, government-verified identity.
  • Asynchronous Certificate Generation
    PDF certificates are generated off the request path and delivered automatically. Each is QR-linked to a verification endpoint.

Why Now

The conditions are right for a category-defining platform

Africa's youth bulge

Over 60% of Africa's population is under 25. Youth development spending by governments, multilaterals, and foundations is accelerating — yet the infrastructure to deliver and measure that spending remains primitive.

Digital identity maturity

Nigeria's NIN rollout has passed 100 million enrolments. For the first time, a platform can verify identity at scale without costly third-party integrations, enabling auditable, fraud-resistant programme delivery.

Funder accountability pressure

International development funders increasingly require verifiable impact data before releasing next-tranche funding. Organisations without digital evidence are losing grants they would otherwise win.

Post-COVID digital adoption

Mobile-first internet penetration across Sub-Saharan Africa doubled between 2019 and 2024. Youth audiences expect digital-first experiences — paper certificates and Excel registers are no longer acceptable.

No direct competitors

Existing tools (Eventbrite, Teachable, LinkedIn) address isolated problems for Western markets. YLSH is the only platform that covers the full programme lifecycle for the African youth development context.

Network-effect flywheel

Each participant's verified profile and certificate history makes them more valuable on the platform. Each mentor, event, and opportunity listed makes the platform more valuable to participants. Value compounds with scale.
